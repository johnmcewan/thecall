Process.

"History is not merely what happened, but what remains in the pockets of the forgotten."

Monday Nite Footballers is more than a database. It is a meticulous reconstruction of the 1970–1979 era of local sports, extracted from thousands of microfiche scans and handwritten playbooks discovered in the basements of community centers across the Midwest.

Behind the Lens

Our primary source was the Kansas City Call, a weekly paper that covered the leagues other major outlets ignored. Every Tuesday morning throughout the 70s, sports editors would transcribe play-by-play data gathered from telephone calls with coaches.

Recovery required a custom-built optical character recognition (OCR) engine tuned specifically for the faded, high-contrast ink of 50-year-old newsprint.

Stage One: Discovery

The Kansas City Call Archives

In 2021, our collective was granted access to the digitized Microfilm collection of the Call. We began by cataloging every sports headline between September and December for the years 1970–1979.

12,400 Scans 92 Cities Covered
Stage Two: Cross-Reference

Validating the Stats

Raw data from the papers was cross-referenced with team rosters provided by high school alumni associations. This allowed us to resolve spelling discrepancies and identify players who went on to professional careers.

Stage Three: Curation

The Digital Folio

Finally, the data is translated into this folio—preserving the aesthetic of the 1970s broadcast while providing modern tools for search and research.
